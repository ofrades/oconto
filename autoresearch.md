# autoresearch.md

## Goal

Write a complete epic poem in Portuguese about the Bible — the story of God — structured as a series of cantos, produced one at a time until the full work is done.

## Vision

The Bible is the greatest story ever told: the real story that all myths and fairy tales shadow. This epic should treat it with the full weight of the classical tradition — in the manner of Homer, Virgil, and Camões — and with the wonder Tolkien identified in fairy stories: recovery, escape, and eucatastrophe. The language is Portuguese in the spirit of Camões. The form is the epic. The matter is Scripture.

## Epic Constraints

This must operate as a true classical-Christian epic, not merely a verse paraphrase of Scripture.

- The whole work must consciously employ epic architecture: proposition of the theme, invocation for aid, elevated style, formal speeches, set-piece scenes, similes, prophetic foreshadowing, and a sense of large providential design.
- The poem must reckon seriously with **in media res**. The final architecture should not feel like a flat chronicle from Genesis forward; it should open with epic force from within the great action, then use recollection, prophecy, narration, or embedded retelling to unfold what came before and after.
- Canto I and the opening movement of the whole work should eventually read unmistakably as an epic proem: proposition, invocation, and statement of the poem’s scope and burden.
- Each canto should be shaped as poetry first: dramatic entrances, speeches, councils, scenes of journey, trial, combat, judgment, hospitality, lament, or revelation — not mere summary.
- Pagan machinery must not govern the poem. The true epic order here is providential: God, angels, demons, covenant, prophecy, worship, fall, judgment, exile, redemption, resurrection.
- The style should remain in Portuguese under the strong formal and rhetorical influence of Camões, while the matter remains faithful to Scripture.
- If earlier cantos fail these epic requirements fully, they should be revised later rather than left as merely serviceable narrative links.

## Data

The following texts are in `./data/` and must be studied before writing:

- [**Lusíadas**](./data/lusiadas.txt) (Camões) — the formal and linguistic model
- [**Vulgate**](./data/vulgate.pdf) (Jerome) — the source and substance of the story
- [**On Fairy-Stories**](./data/on-fairy-stories1.pdf) (Tolkien) — the philosophy of why this story must be told as story

## Output

Produce cantos sequentially: `canto_I.md`, `canto_II.md`, etc. Each canto includes a prose argument and the verses. Continue until the epic is complete.
