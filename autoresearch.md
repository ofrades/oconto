# autoresearch.md

## Goal

Reduce derivative imitation of Camões while preserving true Camonian form.

The immediate task is to revise the opening movement of the poem — especially `canto_I.md` — so it no longer feels like a near-copy of the opening gestures of *Os Lusíadas*. After that, continue a canto-by-canto pass for **manual caesura / accent-pattern / originality correction**, keeping the architecture, theology, and high register, but removing overly obvious Camonian calques.

## Vision

The Bible is the greatest story ever told: the true story of God, creation, fall, covenant, incarnation, redemption, resurrection, Church, and final glory. This epic must bear the weight of Homer, Virgil, and Camões, but it must not merely mimic Camões at the level of opening gestures or rhetorical scaffolding. It should sound like a **new Christian epic in Portuguese**, not a baptized ventriloquism of *Os Lusíadas*. Tolkien still governs the spiritual atmosphere; Camões governs formal grandeur; Vieira may reinforce gravity and sacred eloquence.

## Aesthetic Law

This poem is a Christian epic: **Camões in form, Scripture in substance, Tolkien in spirit, Vieira in selective rhetorical reinforcement — but no servile copying**.

- Keep the corrected macro-architecture: Annunciation opening, Resurrection final eucatastrophe.
- Preserve ottava rima and elevated diction.
- Avoid opening moves that feel like direct recasting of Camões’s first stanzas.
- Prefer original proposition, invocation, and scene-entry while remaining recognizably epic.
- Distinguish **influence** from **imitation**: the poem may echo Camões in cadence, pressure, and dignity, but should not lean on obvious paraphrase or structural cloning.
- Where useful, use more compact syntax and caesural balance to support both originality and meter.

## Formal Constraints

- Maintain ottava-rima tendency, ideally ABABABCC.
- Keep compact heroic cadence.
- Audit for direct or near-direct Camonian calques, especially at canto openings.
- When a passage feels too derivative, rewrite for fresh imagery, syntax, and rhetorical movement rather than merely swapping synonyms.

## Structural Commitments

Keep the established structure:
1. `canto_I.md` — Proémio e Anunciação
2. `canto_II.md` — Advento oculto
3. `canto_III.md` — Manifestação do Filho
4. `canto_IV.md` — O Reino em combate
5. `canto_V.md` — O livro das origens lembrado
6. `canto_VI.md` — Êxodo, Lei e Reino
7. `canto_VII.md` — Profetas, exílio e vigília
8. `canto_VIII.md` — Jerusalém e a Páscoa iminente
9. `canto_IX.md` — Cruz, sepulcro e noite extrema
10. `canto_X.md` — Ressurreição, Emaús, Espírito e consumação
